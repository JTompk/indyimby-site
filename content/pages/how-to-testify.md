---
title: How to testify (and actually move votes)
---

Public hearings are decided by who shows up. Opponents always show up.
Here's how supporters do it well.

## Before the hearing

1. Find the case on the [tracker](https://map.indyimby.com) and read the
   agenda entry — know the case number, the request, and the staff
   recommendation.
2. Hearings are held at the City-County Building (200 E. Washington St.)
   — check the agenda for room and time. Most allow remote participation.
   <!-- TODO: verify current remote-participation procedure -->
3. Sign up to speak when you arrive. You usually get two minutes.

## What to say

- **Say who you are and where you live.** Proximity is credibility:
  "I live four blocks away, and I support this."
- **Be concrete.** One real reason beats five abstractions: the empty lot,
  the coffee shop that needs customers, the kid who could walk to school.
- **Support the ask, not just the vibe.** Name the case number and say
  "I ask you to approve."
- **Stay gracious.** Commissioners remember reasonable people.

## If you can't attend

Written comments count. Email the case planner listed on the agenda before
the hearing date and reference the case number.
